Who I Am

I’m interested in the intersection of storytelling, technology, and organizing. A lot of the projects I work on start as ideas connected to communities, media, or social issues, and I enjoy turning those ideas into something visual, interactive, or informative. Over the past few years, I’ve worked on projects involving journalism, political outreach, event organizing, exhibitions, and digital media. Through that work, I’ve developed skills in web design, writing, graphic design, content creation, and communication. I’m especially interested in how technology and design can make information more engaging and accessible. Some of the tools I use include HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Python, Figma, Photoshop, Canva, and video editing software. I also enjoy experimenting with web designs, UI concepts, and creative python projects. Outside of programming and design, I’m involved in youth organizing and community-based work, which has shaped the way I approach collaboration and storytelling. Many of the projects in this portfolio are still evolving ideas, but they reflect the kinds of things I want to continue building in the future. My goal is to keep exploring opportunities in technology, media, and communications while creating work that combines creativity with real-world impact.
